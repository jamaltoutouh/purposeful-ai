Overview

AI systems increasingly operate in society: they interact with people, institutions, and environments over time, under changing contexts and real constraints. This workshop focuses on purposeful AI, where objectives go beyond predictive accuracy and include human-centered requirements (e.g., safety, fairness, accessibility, trust, accountability) and sustainability goals (e.g., energy efficiency, emissions reduction, climate resilience, resource-aware learning).

The workshop brings together researchers and practitioners working on Evolutionary Computation (EC) and Machine Learning (ML)—individually or in combination—to design, optimize, and evaluate intelligent systems that must navigate multi-objective trade-offs, non-stationarity, heterogeneity, and deployment constraints.

Co-located event: This workshop will take place during FLICS 2026. Please refer to the main conference website for venue details and registration information.

Topics of interest

The workshop is intentionally broad. We welcome methodological and application-driven contributions, including (but not limited to):

EC + ML methods

  • Multi-objective optimization and Pareto-front analysis for learning systems
  • Constrained and safe optimization (hard constraints, feasibility-first, penalty methods)
  • Evolutionary hyperparameter optimization, AutoML, and neural architecture search
  • Population-based training, coevolutionary learning, and memetic EC–ML hybrids
  • Evolutionary reinforcement learning and policy search
  • Surrogate-assisted optimization and simulation-based optimization

Human-centered and trustworthy AI

  • Fairness-aware learning, bias mitigation, and equity metrics
  • Accessibility-by-design and inclusive intelligent services
  • Interpretability, transparency, and human-understandable policies/models
  • Robustness, calibration, reliability, and uncertainty estimation
  • Human-in-the-loop optimization, preference learning, interactive EC/ML
  • Auditability and evaluation protocols for real deployments

Sustainability and resource-aware AI

  • Energy- and carbon-aware training, inference, and deployment
  • Green AI metrics and optimization under compute/energy constraints
  • Sustainability objectives (emissions, resilience, resource allocation)
  • Climate-risk modeling and decision support under uncertainty
  • Trade-offs: performance vs. energy vs. latency vs. robustness

Applications and real-world settings

  • Smart cities and urban computing (mobility, safety, accessibility, energy, sensing)
  • Healthcare and public health decision support
  • Industry 4.0, robotics, predictive maintenance
  • Education and digital public services
  • Environmental monitoring and sustainability analytics
  • Distributed/federated and edge settings (welcome but not required)

Submissions

What we invite

  • Regular papers (research contributions)
  • Short papers / position papers (work-in-progress, visions)
  • Demo / benchmark papers (tools, datasets, evaluation frameworks)
  • Applied case studies (industry/public-sector deployments)

Review criteria include technical quality, clarity, relevance to the workshop scope, and sound evaluation.
